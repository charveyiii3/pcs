Tracking & Updates

How do I track my pet's current location?

We offer two convenient tracking methods:

  • Airway Bill Tracking: Use your airline waybill number for flight status and location updates
  • GPS Device Tracking: Real-time location tracking through our internal GPS monitoring devices with updates every 2-3 seconds

Access our tracking system through the "Track My Pet" button on our website or contact our support team.

Can I get updates during my pet's journey?

Absolutely! We provide comprehensive journey updates including:

  • Departure and arrival confirmations
  • Real-time location updates every few hours
  • Health and comfort checks at each stop
  • SMS and email notifications for major milestones
  • 24/7 access to our customer support team

Our premium service includes photo updates when possible to give you peace of mind throughout the journey.

Can I track multiple pets on one booking?

Yes! Our system supports multi-pet tracking for families traveling with multiple companions. Each pet receives an individual tracking ID, but you can view all pets from a single dashboard. This is especially convenient for military families or those relocating with multiple pets.

Can I track my pet in real-time?

Yes, through our GPS device tracking system. Our advanced tracking technology provides location updates every 2-3 seconds with unlimited global range. The system also monitors your pet's health metrics including heart rate, respiratory rate, and activity levels during transport.

Requirements & Documentation

What documents are required for international transport?

International transport requires several key documents:

  • Veterinary Health Certificate: Issued by a USDA-accredited veterinarian within 10 days of travel
  • USDA APHIS Endorsement: Processed electronically through the VEHCS system
  • Vaccination Records: Including rabies vaccination (timing varies by destination)
  • Microchip Certification: ISO-compliant 15-digit microchip required before rabies vaccination
  • Import Permits: Required by certain destination countries

Our team handles all documentation to ensure compliance with international regulations.

What vaccinations does my pet need?

Vaccination requirements vary by destination, but typically include:

  • Rabies: Mandatory for most international destinations
  • DHPP (Dogs): Distemper, Hepatitis, Parvovirus, Parainfluenza
  • FVRCP (Cats): Feline Viral Rhinotracheitis, Calicivirus, Panleukopenia
  • Special treatments: Tapeworm treatment for Finland, Ireland, Malta, Norway, and Northern Ireland

All vaccinations must be current and administered by a licensed veterinarian. We provide a detailed vaccination schedule based on your destination.

Do you provide crates for transport?

You will need to provide your own IATA-approved transport crate for your pet's journey. We provide detailed guidance on:

  • Proper sizing: How to measure your pet and calculate correct crate dimensions
  • Where to purchase: Recommended retailers and suppliers
  • IATA requirements: What specifications to look for when shopping
  • Preparation tips: How to acclimate your pet and prepare the crate

Contact us for comprehensive sizing guides and purchasing recommendations.

Do you handle exotic pets or just dogs and cats?

While we specialize in dogs and cats, we do handle select exotic pets including birds, rabbits, and small reptiles. Each exotic pet transport requires special permits and custom planning. Contact us for a consultation about your specific pet's needs, as requirements vary significantly by species and destination.

What if my pet has special medical needs?

We specialize in special needs pet transport with:

  • Medical transport services: Climate-controlled vehicles with medical equipment
  • Veterinary escorts: Available for pets requiring medical supervision
  • Modified equipment: Wheelchairs, slings, and specialized carriers
  • Emergency medical transport: Portable oxygen and emergency supplies

Our team works with your veterinarian to create a customized transport plan.

What's the difference between cabin and cargo transport?

Cabin Transport:

  • Pet stays with you in the passenger cabin
  • Size limit: Maximum 18" x 11" x 11" carrier
  • Weight limit: Combined pet and carrier typically 20-40 pounds
  • Soft-sided carriers required by most airlines

Cargo Transport:

  • Pet travels in pressurized, temperature-controlled cargo area
  • Weight limit: Maximum 150 pounds (pet + crate)
  • IATA-approved rigid crates required
  • Professional handling by trained cargo staff

What quarantine requirements should I know about?

Quarantine requirements vary by destination:

  • Hawaii: 5-day or 120-day quarantine depending on preparation
  • UK: No quarantine if EU Pet Passport requirements met
  • Australia/New Zealand: 10-30 days minimum quarantine
  • Japan: 12 hours to 180 days depending on documentation

We coordinate all quarantine arrangements and provide detailed preparation timelines.

How do I know which vaccinations are current?

Check your pet's vaccination record for:

  • Rabies: Must be at least 21 days old and not expired
  • Core vaccinations: Annual boosters for most vaccines
  • Titer testing: May substitute for some vaccinations in certain countries

Our team reviews all vaccination records and advises on any updates needed for your destination.
